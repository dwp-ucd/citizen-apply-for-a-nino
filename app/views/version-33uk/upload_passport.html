Take and upload a photo of your passport

  1. Get your passport ready

    2. Hold your passport open to the pages that include your picture.

    ! WarningIf you have a visa in your passport, make sure you are not taking a photo of the page that shows your vignette.



  2. Take a photo

    3. Take a photo of both pages using a smartphone, tablet or digital camera. You can get someone else to take the photo for you.

    Do not edit your photo. This includes changing the colours and using filters.

    Make sure that:

    • all four corners are visible
    • the photo is in focus
    • the details on your passport are clear enough to read

    Help with taking photos

    If you are struggling to keep your passport open to the relevant pages, you can hold it open. Your hand can be visible in the photo, provided it does not cover any of the text or images.

    To avoid light reflection, take the photo in a bright room and turn the flash off.




  3. Check the photo

    4. Make sure that the photo meets the requirements listed above.


  4. Save the photo to the device you are using to apply

    5. We will ask you to upload these photos at the end of your online application. You should save them to a memorable location on the device you’re using to apply for a National Insurance number.

    Note that we can only accept *.JPG, *.JPEG and *.PNG formats. Save your photo in one of these formats.



Help with transferring photos to the device you are using to apply

You may have taken the photo on a smartphone, but be using a different device (for example, a laptop) to apply for a National Insurance number. In this example, you would need to transfer the photo to the laptop you are using to apply.

You can:

  • email the photo to yourself, and open and save it on the new device
  • send it via Bluetooth®
  • use a cable to link the two devices
