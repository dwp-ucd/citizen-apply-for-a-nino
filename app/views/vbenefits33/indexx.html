Before you start

You should only apply for a National Insurance number if the Department for Work and Pensions (DWP) or the Department for Communities (DfC) told you to do so.


Find your identity documents

If you have the following documents, get them ready before you start:

  • a passport from any country
  • a biometric residence permit (BRP)
  • a national identity card from an EU country or from Norway, Liechtenstein or Switzerland

You may need to enter information from one or more of these documents â€“ for example, your passport number and your BRP number, if you have these.

If you do not have any of these identity documents, you can still apply online for a National Insurance number. However, you will need to prove your identity in a different way, which will take longer. We will contact you after you have applied to tell you what to do.



Prepare to take digital photos

You may need to take digital photographs of your documents and of yourself, and upload these as part of your application. Do not take these photographs yet: we will explain what you need to do.

If you have a smartphone, tablet or a digital camera, make sure these are charged and working before you start.

If you cannot upload photos, you can still apply online for a National Insurance number. However, you will need to prove your identity in a different way, which will take longer. We will contact you after you have applied to tell you what you need to do.
