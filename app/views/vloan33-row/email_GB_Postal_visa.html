! WarningWe cannot make a decision about your application until we receive these photocopies

What you need to do next


  1. 1. Make a note of your application reference number:

    • B0048572

      This is the reference number for your application.
      It is not your National Insurance number.

  2. 2. Make a black and white photocopy of the document you used to apply to the EU Settlement Scheme or for a UK visa:

      • if you used your passport to apply to the EU Settlement Scheme or for a UK visa, photocopy the page that includes your photograph and your signature
      • if you used your national identity card to apply to the EU Settlement Scheme, photocopy both sides of the card

  3. 3. Print the CA5400D form from here.

    • Note that your application reference number (B0048572) is entered automatically on page 1 of the form.

  4. 4. Generate a new immigration status share code for your application.

    • A share code:

      • proves your immigration status
      • consists of 9 digits, for example: A1234567Z
      • is different from any reference number you have been given
      • is different from your National Insurance number

      Share codes are only valid for 30 days. Generate a new code for this application and then send it to us immediately, following steps 5-8 below.

      To generate a share code:

      • go to www.gov.uk/view-prove-immigration-status - this will open in a new tab
      • follow the instructions to view and prove your immigration status
      • when you see what do you need this share code for, select
        something else

  5. 5. Fill in the rest of the form with a black pen using BLOCK CAPITALS.

  6. 6. Write your new share code on page 2 of the form.

  7. 7. Put the completed CA5400D form and your photocopied document in an envelope.

  8. 8. Add the correct postage and send it to:

    • Glasgow NINo Centre
      Mail Handling Site A
      Wolverhampton
      WV98 1BR


We aim to contact you within 16 weeks of receiving your photocopied identity documents to tell you if we have approved your application.

We cannot give you an update on your application before the end of this 16-week period.

We are working hard to process applications that have been delayed because of the coronavirus (COVID-19) pandemic. We will review your application as soon as we can.

You can start work and get paid without a National Insurance number if you can prove you can work in the UK. Your employer might ask for proof of your right to work in the UK.
