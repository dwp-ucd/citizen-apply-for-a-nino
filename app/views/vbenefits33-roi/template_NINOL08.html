Apply for a National Insurance number


Dear, {{ data['given-names'] }} {{ data['family-name'] }}

You recently asked for a National Insurance number (NINo).
We are inviting you to apply using our online service.


What you need to do


Step 1
Apply online at:
https://apply-national-insurance-number.dwp.gov.uk

Complete your application as soon as possible. You are the only person who can use the online service with your details. Please do not share this web address with anyone else.

At the end of your application, you will get an 8-digit application reference number: for example, B1234567. Make a note of this number and keep it safe.



Step 2
Make photocopies of your identity documents.

Make a black and white photocopy of one of the following documents:

  • the personal details pages of your passport, including your photograph and signature or
  • both sides of your national identity card

You told us that you have settled or pre-settled status under the European Union Settlement Scheme. You need to generate a share code and write it on page 2 of the CA5400 form.


A share code:

  • consists of 9 digits, for example A1234567Z
  • is different from your EU Settlement Scheme application reference number
  • is different from your National Insurance number

Your share code is only valid for 30 days. Please generate a new share code for this application.


To do this:

  • go to www.gov.uk/view-prove-immigration-status
  • follow the instructions to view and prove your immigration status
  • when you see ‘what do you need this share code for’, select ‘something else’
  • write the share code on page 2 of the CA5400 form

If you do not have settled or pre-settled status under the EUSS, you cannot currently apply for a National Insurance number online. This is because we will not be able to verify your identity.



Step 3
Complete the CA5400 form enclosed with this letter.

You need to write your application reference number on page 2 of the form.

If you have settled or pre-settled status under the European Union Settlement Scheme, you also need to include your share code on page 2 of the form.



Step 4
Post your completed CA5400 form and photocopies of your identity documents to us by 1 January 2000.

Use the envelope provided. You do not need a stamp.

If you do not send us the correct documents by this date, your application could be delayed or refused.



What happens next

We will process your application and write to you with our decision.

If you do not get our decision by 1 January 2000, please contact us on 0000 000 0000. You will need to give us the LMS reference number on the first page of this letter.


Yours sincerely,

Office manager




Equality and Diversity

We treat people fairly, regardless of their disability, ethnicity, gender, sexual orientation, transgender status, marital or civil partnership status, age, religion or beliefs.

Call charges

Calls to 0800 numbers are free from personal mobiles and landlines.

Why DWP needs personal information and how we treat it

We treat personal information carefully. We may use it for any of our purposes. To learn more about information rights and how we use information, please see our DWP Personal Information Charter at www.gov.uk/dwp/personal-information-charter.
