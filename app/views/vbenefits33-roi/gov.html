Apply for a National Insurance number

You need a National Insurance number to:

  • work
  • receive benefits
  • receive a student loan
  • pay Class 3 voluntary National Insurance contributions so you can get a state pension when you retire

You can start work without a National Insurance number if you can prove you have the right to work in the UK. You can also apply for benefits or a student loan.


This service is also available in Welsh (Cymraeg).

Who can apply using this service

You can apply for a National Insurance number if you:

  • live in England, Scotland or Wales
  • have the right to work in the UK
  • are working or looking for work
  • have never had a National Insurance number before

You cannot currently apply for a National Insurance number in Northern Ireland because of coronavirus (COVID-19).

Before you apply

You do not need to use this service if you:

  • have lost your National Insurance number
  • are a UK resident aged 19 or under
  • have a biometric residence permit (BRP) with a National Insurance number on the back
  • are applying for benefits or a student loan

If you’re a UK resident aged 19 or under

You should have received a National Insurance number in the 3 months before your 16th birthday.

If you are aged 16 to 19 and did not receive a National Insurance number, call HM Revenue and Customs.

If you have a biometric residence permit (BRP)

You may already have a National Insurance number on the back of your BRP.

If there is a National Insurance number on your BRP, you can start using it straight away.

If there is no National Insurance number on your BRP, you can apply for one using this service.

If you’re applying for benefits or a student loan

You do not need to apply for a National Insurance number separately

You will receive a National Insurance number if your application for benefits or a student loan is successful.

After you apply

We will tell you what you need to do next.

If you have a Biometric Residence Permit (BRP) or have EU Settled Status, we will ask you to send us proof of your identity. Otherwise, we will tell you how to make an appointment with us.

We aim to make a decision within 4 weeks after:

  • you attend an appointment with us
  • or we receive your proof of identity
