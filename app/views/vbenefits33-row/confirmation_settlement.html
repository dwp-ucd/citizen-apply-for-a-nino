You now need to send us photocopies of your identity documents

We have received your application for a National Insurance number.


! Warning We cannot make a decision about your application until we receive photocopies of your identity documents

What you need to do next


  1. 1. Make a note of this reference number:

    • B0048572

      This is the reference number for your application.
      It is not your National Insurance number.

  2. 2. Make a black and white photocopy of one of the following documents:

      • If you are using your passport to apply for a National Insurance number, photocopy the page that includes your photograph and your signature
      • If you are using your national identity card to apply for a National Insurance number, photocopy both sides of the card

  3. 3. Generate a new EU Settlement Scheme share code for your application.

    • A share code:

      • consists of 9 digits, for example: A1234567Z
      • is different from your EU Settlement Scheme application reference number
      • is different from your National Insurance number

      Share codes are only valid for 30 days. Generate a new code for this application and then send it to us immediately, following steps 4-7 below.

      To generate a share code:

      • go to www.gov.uk/view-prove-immigration-status - this will open
        in a new tab
      • follow the instructions to view and prove your immigration status
      • when you see what do you need this share code for, select
        something else

  4. 4. Fill in the CA5400D form.

    • Print the form and fill it in with a pen. Use BLOCK CAPITALS.

  5. 5. Write your application reference number (B0048572) on page one of the form.

  6. 6. Write your new share code on page two.

  7. 7. Add the correct postage and send it to:

    • Glasgow NINO Centre
      Mail Handling Site A
      Wolverhampton
      WV98 1BR


If we approve your application, we will send you a National Insurance number. We aim to post this to you within 4 weeks of receiving your photocopied identity documents.

You can start work and get paid without a National Insurance number if you can prove you can work in the UK. Your employer might ask for proof of your right to work in the UK.
